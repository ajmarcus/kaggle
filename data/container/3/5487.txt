"The author of Winter Hibiscus made a good choice to conclude the story with when they come back, Saeng vowed silently to herself, in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus  is budding, then I will take that test again. This paragraph hints to the reader that Saeng intends to combine her new and old lives. Her old life is represented by the hibiscus; she used to the constants from back home. The geese symbolize her new life; they have become almost familiar to her now The obstacle she faces is passing the driving test. The author implies that in the spring, when the old and new parts of Saengs life are combined, Saeng will be able to overcome her obstacle, and all will be well. With the geese in the ground, Saengs life will have come full circle."
